WHEREAS

A. The Company is engaged in the business of offering admission and visa counselling for overseas education in Universities.

B. The RP has approached the Company to offer counselling services with the intent to recruit students for enrolment in the Universities in the United States, U.K., New Zealand, Australia, Canada and other destinations through the Company.

C. Now, therefore, the Parties are executing this Agreement to record their mutual understanding relating to the a fore-mentioned arrangement, the terms and conditions thereof and other incidental matters arising therefrom.

2. RIGHTS OF RP

2.1. The Company hereby grants RP the right to market and offer in the Territory where the RP operates, and any other territories as agreed by the Parties in writing.

2.2. Subject to Clause 9 (Non-Solicitation), the parties expressly acknowledge that this Agreement does not create an exclusive relationship between the Parties

3. Representations and Warranties of RP
The RP hereby represents and warrants to the Company that:
3.1.all documents received from prospective students are / will be authentic and true. The Company may ask for original or duly notarised documents from the RP.
3.2. it has not and shall not:
  • 3.2.1. engage in dishonest practices.
  • 3.2.2. mislead a student by making unrealistic suggestions about studying in any of the Company’s Destination Markets.
  • 3.2.3. facilitate the enrolment of an international student who does not comply with the conditions of a student visa.
  • 3.2.4. engage in false or misleading advertising and recruitment practices.
The RP acknowledges that doing so may result in the termination of this agreement.
3.3. there are no proceedings, pending, threatened or decided, against staff, directors, contractor, or other parties related to the RP who are directly or indirectly involved in the operation of services related to the Company or this Agreement.
4. RESPONSIBILITIES OF RP
The RP agrees and undertakes that it shall:
4.1. comply with and act in accordance with the Code of Conduct and Code of Practice;
4.2. use the Company’s platform and services to enrol students into the Universities and/or provide related services according to the terms and conditions herein.
4.3. act with diligence and in a professional manner and use its best commercial endeavours to fulfil the duties described herein and maintain reasonable technical and practical knowledge regarding the Services.
4.4. use promotional materials provided by the Company from time to time solely for the purpose of using, marketing, and promoting the Services.
4.5. submit enrolment ready applications or referral leads to the Company for the purpose of application and admission into the Universities.
4.6. ensure that all required application fees and charges related to the application are paid directly to the University or to the Company, at the discretion of and upon directions issued by the Company in writing.
4.7. comply with all relevant legislation, applicable laws and policies relating to the recruitment of students in Destination Markets and Universities.
4.8. ensure that all the relevant University’s policies are accurately explained to a prospective student prior to acceptance of offer from the same. This includes but is not limited to the following:
  • 4.8.1. Refund policies
  • 4.8.2. Successful completion of enrolment
  • 4.8.3. Visa application refusal or visa grant cancellation
  • 4.8.4. Transfer to another provider
  • 4.8.5. Early cessation of their studies
  • 4.8.6. Termination of studies for non-payment of fees or disciplinary reasons
  • 4.8.7. Unsatisfactory course progress or course attendance
  • 4.8.8. Deferral or suspension of studies
4.9. not claim or purport that the agreements by the Company with Universities are the agreements entered into by the RP, unless the RP has, in its own capacity, signed an agreement directly with such university or college.
4.10. not advertise, use, or promote University’s logos or branded materials without the direct approval of the Company or concerned University in writing.
5. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES OF THE COMPANY
The Company shall endeavour that:
5.1. the application for study placement to various Universities will not be delayed unnecessarily for reasons in the Company’s control.
5.2. the Company will process all commission within 30 days after receipt of payment from the concerned University and the subsequent receipt from the RP of a complete and accurate invoice in accordance with Clause 6 hereof.
6. COMMISSION
6.1. The Company shall pay to the RP commission where the RP sources the student in accordance with this Agreement and provides an enrolment-ready application.
6.2. Upon the Company receiving commission from the concerned University in relation to the enrolment of the relevant student, the Company shall intimate the RP in writing of the same.
6.3. The Company shall pay the RP a commission at a percentage of (“Commission”), in relation to the commission received by the Company in Clause 6.2. Exact Commercials have been shared with you over email. This is subject to periodic revision subject to Company’s discretion. Any changes in the commission structure will always be notified by email. Provided however that, if the student, for any reason, does not pay the required tuition fees / or abandons the program for which admission has been procured, no remuneration shall be payable by the Company to the RP.
6.4. Upon the RP receiving intimation from the Company in accordance with Clause 6.2 hereto, the RP shall promptly, and in no event later than 5 days from receipt of such notice, raise an invoice on the Company for payment of the Commission.
6.5. Invoices raised by the RP shall have the following information:
  • 6.5.1. Name of the student
  • 6.5.2. Institution ID of the student
  • 6.5.3. Name of the institution
  • 6.5.4. Title of the course
  • 6.5.5. Semester being claimed
  • 6.5.6. Amount due
  • 6.5.7. Full bank details of RP

8. FRAUD AND DISHONEST PRACTICES
8.1. The Company shall take immediate corrective and preventive action if it becomes aware of the RP being negligent, careless, or incompetent or being engaged in false, misleading, or unethical advertising and recruitment practices including practices that could harm the integrity of the student and/or University. The RP has acknowledged under Clause 3, that documents submitted for the purpose of Services are not fraudulent and will not place the student’s integrity at risk.
8.2. In the event the Company is of the opinion that any fraud and/or dishonest practice has been committed by RP, the Company may terminate this Agreement in accordance with Clause 10.
9. NON-SOLICITATION
During the term of this Agreement, and for 12 months after any termination of this Agreement, the RP will not use its relationship or reference activities conducted with the Company to directly contact any University for the explicit purpose of entering a commercial agreement. This excludes those institutions that the RP has represented previously or with whom the RP, at the time of this Agreement, has an existing agreement.
10. TERM AND TERMINATION
10.1. This Agreement shall commence upon the date of execution and continue for an initial period of 2 years, unless terminated under Clause 8.2 or the Company cancels this Agreement in writing by giving 30 days' notice at the end of the first and subsequent terms.
10.2. Either Party may terminate this agreement immediately by giving written notice to the other Party if:
  • 10.2.1. the other Party commits a material breach of the agreement which, if capable of remedy, has not been remedied within 30 days after written notice is given to the other Party;
  • 10.2.2. the other Party ceases to hold a relevant licence or authorisation required in connection with this agreement under relevant legislation;
  • 10.2.3. the other Party has taken any step towards its winding up, dissolution or administration, compromise or arrangement with any third party (including creditors); or
  • 10.2.4. the other Party engages in conduct which causes or is likely to cause damage to the reputation of the Party, or its related entities or business partners, clients, or customers.
10.3. Upon termination of this Agreement, the RP shall cease marketing and offering the Services and shall continue to abide by the obligation to refrain from sharing with any third party any of the Company’s Confidential Information in accordance with Clause 11 below.
10.4. Unless terminated, this Agreement will automatically renew for a further period of 1 year.
11. CONFIDENTIALITY
11.1. Confidential information (“Confidential Information”) shall mean any information disclosed by Company (“Disclosing Party”) to the RP (“Receiving Party”), in any form including without limitation documents, business plans, technical/financial/marketing/customer/business information, specifications, analysis, designs, drawings, data, computer programs, any information relating to personnel or affiliates of a party and include information disclosed by third parties at the direction of a Disclosing Party and marked as confidential within 15 days of such disclosure.
11.2. The RP shall, during the subsistence of this Agreement and at all times thereafter, not disclose to any Person, directly or indirectly, the Confidential Information pertaining to the Company. The RP shall be entitled to disclose such Confidential Information only after obtaining the prior written permission from the Company.
11.3. The RP shall not be obligated to maintain confidentiality in respect of the Confidential Information which:
  • 11.3.1. is known to the general public at large at the time of disclosure as is evidenced by written records;
  • 11.3.2. becomes known to the RP from a source other than the Company who possesses the Confidential Information legally and without restriction on disclosure;
  • 11.3.3. is in the public domain or becomes in the public domain through no wrongful act of RP;
  • 11.3.4. is disclosed with the prior written approval of the Company; and
  • 11.3.5. is disclosed pursuant to any judicial or governmental request, requirement, or order, provided that the RP takes reasonable steps to give the Company sufficient prior notice in order to contest such request, requirement or order.
11.4. The RP hereby agrees to keep secret and confidential the proprietary information made available to it during the subsistence of this Agreement. The RP will take reasonable measures to maintain the confidentiality of the Company’s Confidential Information, but never less than the standard of care that an ordinary prudent business would exercise to maintain the secrecy of its own Confidential Information.
